Mont Ventoux — complete guide to hiking · cultural discovery · eco-tourism in the "Giant of Provence."

## Territory snapshot
Mont Ventoux peaks at **1 910 m**, dominates Vaucluse between Alps and Mediterranean. UNESCO Biosphere Reserve since 1990. Hosts **1 500+** plant species · 150 nesting bird species · 2 500+ insect species. Regional Natural Park (est. 2020): **877 km²** across 37 communes.

## Hikes: all levels
### Easy / family
- Sentier Jean-Henri Fabre (Mont Serein): two loops (1h · 2h), fauna/flora interpretation signage, UNESCO biosphere, child-friendly
- Bois du Défends, Sault: circuits of 6.7 km · 9.4 km in shaded forest, picnic tables, lavender fields in season
- Sentier des Demoiselles Coiffées: ~1h, unique rock formations, easy · accessible
- Rocher du Midi, Gigondas: 5 km loop (1h45), belvedere at **468 m**, views on Ventoux · Dentelles de Montmirail · Rhône valley

### Summit ascent (sport)
- North face from Mont Serein: shortest route (4.3 km one-way), demanding climb, alpine feel — fir · beech
- South face from Bédoin: classic mythic route (+**1 463 m** gain), cedar forests → mineral lunar desert above **1 800 m**

## Eco-tourism and nature
### Wildlife observation
Massif hosts deer · roe deer · chamois · mouflon · wild boar · wolves (packs identified in recent years). At dusk, Sentier d'interprétation de la grande faune, Aurel (2 km, equipped for nocturnal outings): listen to red deer rut, observe land-art installations. Emblematic species: golden eagle · Egyptian vulture · Apollo butterfly (threatened mountain species).

### Gorges de la Nesque
Natura 2000 site. Canyon 12 km long, up to **400 m** deep, surrounded by lavender · spelt. Loop of 8.8 km (3h) from Monieux: crosses wetlands · garrigue · old-growth forest, incl. Romanesque cave chapel Saint-Michel d'Anesca (12th c.).

### Jardin Singulier & Vallée du Toulourenc
In wild north-face valley near Saint-Léger-du-Ventoux: thematic paths (botany · geology · art), nature bookshop, kitchen-garden restaurant, exhibitions. Eco-responsible via participatory membership.

### Source du Groseau (Malaucène)
Protected Natural Heritage site (Espace Naturel Sensible). Karstic spring at foot of 14th-c. abbey (former summer residence of Pope Clement V). Forest walk to observe treecreeper · Sardinian warbler · short-toed snake eagle.

## Villages
PNR: 11 perched villages with remarkable heritage.

- Sault (766 m): lavender capital, spelt plateau, Provençal market, hiking trailhead
- Bédoin: southern gateway to Ventoux, fortified medieval village, Église Saint-Pierre
- Malaucène: Ventoux capital, medieval heritage, ramparts · fountains
- Brantes: steep medieval village, artist · ceramicist · potter community
- Venasque: "Plus Beaux Villages de France," panorama on Ventoux · Dentelles de Montmirail
- Vaison-la-Romaine: rich antique · medieval heritage, multi-day hiking hub

## Unusual activities and local produce
- Donkey trek: half-day to multi-day bivouac around the massif
- Carriage ride in vineyards: secrets of AOC Ventoux viticulture (appellation since 1973)
- Lavender honey tasting: several beekeepers open to visitors
- Ventoux cycling tour: 128 km in 2–3 days — perched villages · lavender fields · Gorges de la Nesque
- All-terrain scooter · tree-climbing (Mont Serein station, north face)

## Eco-responsible practices
PNR du Mont Ventoux carries the "Valeurs Parc" label certifying producers · service providers committed to sustainable development. Biodiversity under pressure (tourism · urbanization · climate change) — respect Natura 2000 zones and biotope protection orders.

## Vignerons du Mont Ventoux — Bédoin
Most activity-rich producer, steps from south-face trailhead.

- Buggy excursion in vineyards: 2h in automatic buggies (2 pers. max), oldest Bédoin plots with panoramic view, sommelier-guided tasting — 120 €/2 pers.
- Guided vigneron walk: 2h with passionate vigneron on gentle · sustainable viticulture, gourmet tasting — 2 €/pers. (6–20 pers.), reservation 2 weeks ahead
- Tasting workshop: sight · smell · taste on AOC Ventoux wines, year-round

620 route de Carpentras, 84410 Bédoin — Tél. 04 90 65 95 72

## TerraVentoux — Villes-sur-Auzon
Sustainable cooperative (1 000 ha, 15% of AOC Ventoux).

- Vigneron walks in parcels
- E-bike in vineyard to Blauvac village, tasting on arrival — ~19 €/pers.
- Dusk viti-cultural walk with botanist · vigneron, soupe au pistou · Romani songs — ~16 €/pers.
- Truffle tasting: black truffle secrets with local trufficulteur — 2–5 €
- Carriage rides · e-Solex in vines — 19–26 €/pers.

## Domaine des Peyre — Robion
Major wine-tourism estate combining history · modernity, between Gordes and L'Isle-sur-la-Sorgue. Free contemporary art trail on estate, plus tastings from 8 €/adult.

## Domaine de la Camarette — Pied du Ventoux
Family estate: wines · olive oils · sustainable crops, open for thematic tasting visits.

## Maison Gabriel Meffre — Gigondas
Large Côtes du Rhône estate (near Dentelles de Montmirail). Guided tastings of top northern · southern Rhône Valley wines, free entry.

## Wine route — South Ventoux (53 km circuit)
Self-guided route crosses 13 communes (Carpentras · Bédoin · Caromb · Crillon-le-Brave…) with a pedagogical AOC varietal parcel at Bédoin entrance. AOC Ventoux: **5 700 ha** across 51 communes, extending to Monts du Vaucluse. Each autumn: Vignobles en scène (ex-Fascinant Week-end) — gastronomy · immersive tastings across the territory.

## Vaison-la-Romaine (must-see)
Ranked among the 100 most beautiful French detours. 2 000 years of history in one site.

- Puymin · La Villasse antique sites: largest archaeological site open to public in France (15 ha) — luxury domus · baths · mosaic-paved streets · Antique Theatre (still used for summer shows)
- Musée archéologique Théo Desplans: "Musée de France" label, children's workshops (Roman Lego architecture · mosaic)
- Roman bridge (1st c.): still in service after 2 000 years and Ouvèze floods
- Perched medieval city: ramparts · comtal château (1195) · belfry · cobblestone lanes
- Cathédrale Notre-Dame-de-Nazareth: 11th-c. Romanesque with remarkable cloister

~30 km north of Ventoux — guided tours · audioguides available, tél. 04 90 36 50 48

## Venasque (6th–13th c.)
"Plus Beaux Villages de France." One of Provence's oldest Christian sites.

- Merovingian baptistery (6th c.): among oldest religious buildings in France
- Romanesque Église Notre-Dame (13th c.) · three Saracen towers with rampart remains

~20 km southwest of Ventoux

## Séguret (medieval)
"Plus Beaux Villages de France," perched on Dentelles de Montmirail.

- Église Saint-Denis (10th c.) · Mascarons fountain
- Stone-paved lanes · santonnier · craft artisans

~25 km northwest of Ventoux

## Carpentras (papal city)
Papal possession 1229–1791. Rich heritage.

- Roman arch (1st c.): one of few Roman triumphal arches in southern France
- Gothic Cathédrale Saint-Siffrein · 15th-c. synagogue (among oldest in France)

~20 km southwest of Ventoux

## Bédoin — Chapelle de la Madeleine
In the Ventoux access village: Chapelle de la Madeleine (11th c.) — oldest building in village, on the Malaucène road. Baroque Église Saint-Pierre (17th c.) with Jesuit facade dominates the old village.

## Aurel — Château and artist village
Built on rocky outcrop. 12th-c. church · château. Painter refuge (René Seyssaud · Pierre Ambrogiani). Balcony over Sault lavender fields.

## Blauvac — Cistercian abbey
Perched village: château · Cistercian abbey · Chapelle Notre-Dame-des-Neiges. Unobstructed view over Ventoux · Monts de Vaucluse.

## Avignon (UNESCO, ~45 km)
45 km southwest. UNESCO World Heritage: Palais des Papes · medieval ramparts · Pont Saint-Bénézet.

## Must-dos
### Summit ascent
Emblematic experience — on foot from Bédoin (south face, +**1 463 m**, ~4h) or by bike on three mythic faces. Clear day: views to Alps · sea · Pyrenees. Chalet Reynard (south face) · Mont Serein (north face, more wooded · quiet) serve as bases for both sides.

### Gorges de la Nesque — bike or foot
Unanimously cited as top experience. Route des Gorges (D942): canyon **400 m** deep · 12 km between Monieux and Villes-sur-Auzon. By bike: combines perfectly with Sault lavender fields.

## Nature and well-being
- Wildlife at dusk: Aurel trail with naturalist guide — deer · chamois · red deer rut
- Stargazing overnight above Gorges de la Nesque: insolite lodging at Méthamis (B&B with jacuzzi · panoramic view) or barrel accommodation in Vaucluse vineyards
- Multi-day donkey trek in bivouac — especially recommended for families or digital detox

## Sport and thrills
- MTB · gravel in the massif: marked routes from Bédoin all levels, rentals on-site. Grande Traversée de Vaucluse MTB circuit ranked nationally
- All-terrain electric scooter on Ventoux trails — activity trending strongly since 2024
- Mont Serein tree-climbing park: zip lines with valley views, all ages
- Semi-marathon des Gorges de la Nesque (Villes-sur-Auzon) — next edition 10 May 2026

## Terroir and gastronomy
- Harvest + winemaking workshop at TerraVentoux · Vignerons du Mont Ventoux (Bédoin): hands-on · convivial, often cited as best trip memory
- Fruit harvest · olive oil production: several estates open in autumn
- Sault market (every Wednesday morning): most authentic in region — lavender honey · spelt · ewe's cheese · nougat

## Unusual culture
- Village des Bories (near Gordes, ~40 km): prehistoric dry-stone hut settlement, one of Vaucluse's most striking curiosities
- Abbaye Notre-Dame de Sénanque: 12th-c. Cistercian abbey surrounded by lavender, iconic Provence image
- Colorado Provençal de Rustrel: former ochre quarries in red-orange hues, 1–2h walk in otherworldly landscape
